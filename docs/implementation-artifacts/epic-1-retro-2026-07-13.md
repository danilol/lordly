## Successes

1. **The OB64 fantasy already lands on pure mechanics** — the single most important finding: the feel comes from the systems (lanes, draft, reveal, non-intervention battle), so Epic 2 amplifies something real rather than compensating.
2. **Determinism discipline paid compound interest** — golden-snapshot tripwires (est. 1.5), the stream-ordering invariant comment (est. 1.4 review), and deliberately pre-built seams (engagement-scoped coin flip 1.4, `WipeState 'both'` 1.5, no-single-`EngagementEnded` special case 1.9) made the 1.10 stretch genuinely cheap: engagement 1 bit-identical, 5 goldens untouched, scene delta = one label.
3. **Adversarial review caught what property tests couldn't** — 2 HIGH RNG defects (FNV isn't avalanche; affine cross-label aliasing); reviews contributed ~30–40% of several stories' tests. The Blind Hunter layer, not AC-auditing, found the correctness bugs.
4. **Empirical over reasoned** — 1.7's hand-reasoned AI pool failed its own dominance band at 92%; the sim harness + full pairwise matrix produced the shipped pool. House rule since.
5. **The defer pattern held** — every mid-epic product wish (position-dependent moves, flavor text, log panel, wipeout toggle, archer balance) was routed through deferred-work.md / correct-course into scoped work. Zero scope creep; the sole breach (1.2's "coming soon" label) was caught in review.
6. **"Previous story intelligence" worked** — toolchain PATH prefix, deploy gotchas, purity-list mechanism: once recorded in a story's Dev Notes, never re-tripped.

## Challenges

1. **Recurring review findings — a knowledge-vs-enforcement gap.** Hardcoded-values-that-should-be-data flagged **5×** (1.2, 1.6, 1.8 ×2, 1.10); boundary/guard gaps **5×**; untested claims **4×** (still in 1.10); dead/drifted prose **5×**. The lessons were written and cited forward — and still recurred. A rule that fails five times wants to be a lint rule, not a lesson.
2. **Dev test-design errors were the top defect class** until 1.6 — wrong-unit coin-flip pin (1.4), misread damage table (1.5), blast-crossfire surprise (1.6). Root cause: pasted expected values instead of hand-derivation. Fixed by the hand-verified-anchor discipline ("verify WHICH unit acts").
3. **Fix passes introduced new defects twice** (1.1 patch-pass regressions ×2; 1.7's first mirror fix wrong). The re-review net caught every one — review-the-fix is now mandatory, not optional.
4. **Blurry text — reclassified from rendering tech-debt to ACCESSIBILITY.** Danilo: "my not-so-good vision finds it very blurry." 1.8's review dismissed DPR-aware scaling as a "micro-opt"; the real device falsified that. Candidate causes: fixed `TEXT_RESOLUTION = 3` vs `devicePixelRatio` rendering, and font-size floors (10–11px labels at 360w). Acceptance test: *comfortably readable on Danilo's actual device, by Danilo.*

## Key insights

1. Invariant-stating comments become load-bearing (stream-ordering carried story 1.10); intent-describing comments rot. Write the former.
2. One-device feedback is a bottleneck the presentation epic will feel much harder — and that one device's constraints (vision, DPI) are requirements, not edge cases.
3. Pre-building seams for named future stories is cheap insurance that pays off at face value or better.
4. Cross-model / cross-tier review independence matters (noted in 1.7, 1.8, 1.9): reviewer ≠ implementer tier found more.
