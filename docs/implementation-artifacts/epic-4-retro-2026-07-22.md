## What went well

- **The dossier gate worked.** One logVersion bump, planned in 4.1, shipped in 4.2, never needed again — the union walk caught a real design flaw (`attackFlavor` inferring animation from class) *before* the bump froze it. No story stalled on an undesigned rule, which was 4.1's stated purpose.
- **A reusable architecture pattern emerged:** the pure render seam (`eventTrace` 4.10, `movePlate` 4.11) — a pure `BattleEvent → render-intent | null` function, exhaustively tested, consumed by the scene. "Honest rendering" and "one plate per action" hold by construction; both reviews found the seams flawless.
- **Balance tuning went from art to method.** 4.2's naive 5-slot pool broke the band at 86.5%; the deterministic hill-climb over pool variants fixed it, 4.3 found the converging search space (single-unit substitutions), and 4.5/4.6 landed big mechanics with no re-tune — the pool stabilized once structure settled.
- **Device sessions were design sessions, not sign-off ceremonies.** The scrollable picker died on device and became the icon grid (4.3); the melee blockade became a rule mid-story (4.4); the crown moved onto the battle board (4.5); story 4.13 exists because a device session found a dead-end. Corrections happened at the cheapest possible moment.
- **The intake discipline held under pressure:** every PO wish and review defer was logged to deferred-work.md instead of self-scoped mid-story; recorded deviations (interim sprites, 24px targets, nameRolls-as-class-list…) shipped dated and PO-visible instead of as silent drift.
- **Golden/drift-guard discipline:** re-record once, audit event-by-event, cross-check hand derivations, treat any unexpected magic-log diff as a bug — plus `rules-doc.test.ts`, the balance-hash pin, union-keyed Records, and 4.12's new reverse class-coverage guard repeatedly catching coupled edits at build time.

## Challenges / lessons

- **The 4.8 monster saga (the epic's big lesson).** Chartered "pure engine, no device session"; delivered 7 device rounds, two renamed violation codes, one wrongly-generalized adjacency rule (round 6 — inferred from a single screenshot, fully reverted when Danilo pasted the OB64 research verbatim), and a complete model revision (two-cell → single-cell king-move). Root causes, systemic not personal: (a) the dossier's monster design was invented, internally consistent, and **never validated against the source game**; (b) TS exhaustiveness stubs silently made the Golem live in the shell, so "pure engine" was a fiction. The counter-pattern that worked: annotated screenshots + pasted research as authority settled in one round what inference churned on for six.
- **Placement gestures are a bug farm.** Four confirmed review bugs across 4.4/4.5 in the same tap/double-tap/drag layer — every gesture added beside existing gestures produced a race that review (not tests, not the device) caught.
- **Paperwork lags device-iteration churn.** The most recurrent review theme (4.3, 4.4, 4.6, 4.8–4.11): story docs, checkboxes, comments, and planning artifacts contradicting shipped reality after in-session design changes. Never a shipped bug; always review time. The stale-PRD bundle (FR38 monster model, FR17 wipeout default, dragon deferral, AD-2 mid-battle note) is the accumulated debt.
- **The 360px army-row overflow was the epic's signature shell bug** (ResultScene device-caught, HistoryScene review-caught HIGH in 4.2) — graduated into a standing rule: on any army-size/slot change, grep every comp-rendering scene, not just the File List.
- **Two rolling items got formal teeth:** the `pnpm coverage` instrumentation flake ("a gate that only passes on retry is quiet erosion" — 4.8/4.10/4.11) and the thrice-deferred `?perf=1` capture. Both are Epic 5 housekeeping with deadlines.

## Team agreements (new)

- **Source evidence before engine code** — any OB64-fidelity mechanic gets Danilo's reference captures/pasted research at *design* time, and dev stops to ask for source rather than inferring from a single screenshot. Danilo's commitment from the session: more questions asked, clearer definitions given — formalized here as a two-way agreement, not a PO burden.
- **Gesture changes get a gesture audit** — any new tap/drag/double-tap on a scene with existing gestures reviews the full interaction table (timers, thresholds, destroy-vs-dragend lifecycles) before review.

## Key takeaways

1. **Design gates work — but a gate is only as good as its evidence.** The dossier prevented every mid-epic design stall except the one place it designed without source validation (the monster). The new agreement closes that hole.
2. **The device loop is the product's design engine.** Nearly every planned UI was improved or replaced after Danilo touched it, one story was born there, and the melee blockade — a core rule — came from play, not planning. Budget for it; it's not churn.
3. **The game crossed its maturity milestone:** eight new systems landed on one era bump, certified in-band with zero closing tuning, on a test suite that grew 59% — and the bottleneck has moved from building mechanics to polishing the experience (Epic 5) and adding people (Epic 6).
