## Story

As the game's developer,
I want the Golem — a two-cell monster — drafting, placing, and fighting correctly in the pure engine,
so that the monster era is proven deterministic before any pixel renders (story 4.9).

## Acceptance Criteria

1. **The Golem enters balance data as a 2-slot, two-cell `monster` (FR38, FR15, AD-1).** `ALL_CLASSES` gains `'golem'`; `BALANCE.classes.golem` is a full FR15 citizen with `sizeClass: 'monster'`, `role: 'brute'`, the dossier §1 stat row (HP 300, STR 28, VIT 36, INT 4, MEN 8, AGI 4, DEX 10, actions `2/1/1`), and a `moves` entry (uniform melee across all three rows — see Dev Notes for the kind). `SLOT_COST['monster'] === 2` and `slotTotal` already exist (story 4.2) — a Golem costs 2 through them with **no new arithmetic**. `Brute` carries **no** `roleRelations` (dossier §1, D-1e: the magic weakness is pure stats — low MEN — not an Artillery→Brute relation), so `roleRelations` is unchanged. `balanceVersion` bumps (8→9, hash re-pinned). **SCOPE: GOLEM ONLY** — the dragon is deferred to a later wave (dossier D-1b, signed off; the epics/PRD "dragon and golem" wording is STALE — see Dev Notes).

2. **A monster is ONE unit with a stored anchor and an engine-derived footprint (AD-14, FR38, FR4).** A monster's `placements` entry stores a single **anchor cell = the front-most cell of its body**; the second cell is **derived**, never stored, via a new engine-exported `footprintCells(class, anchor): Placement[]` (a small → its one anchor cell; a monster → the anchor plus the cell exactly one row back). Anchor `back` is **illegal** for a monster (the body would leave the grid); a monster's footprint is always two **vertically adjacent cells of one column** (Front+Middle or Middle+Back), **never horizontal**. Every `BattleLog` event references the unit **id**, never a cell (unchanged — AD-14).

3. **Placement legality is extended and has ONE implementation shared with `validateMatchSetup` (AD-14, FR38, FR4).** `validateMatchSetup` grows footprint-aware checks — a monster anchored `back`, two cells of any unit (small or monster) colliding, **two monsters sharing a column**, and **more than 2 monsters** per army are each a **typed, tested `InvalidMatchSetupError`** with a new discriminant code. The engine also exports incremental helpers `legalAnchors(class, army, placements)` and `canPlace(class, anchor, army, placements)` for the placement scene's live drag feedback (story 4.9 consumes them), and they **share their legality implementation** with `validateMatchSetup` — no second copy of FR4's column/verticality rules. (Story 4.9 renders the drag feedback; 4.8 only builds and unit-tests the helpers.)

4. **Two-cell TARGETED semantics behave exactly as the dossier §2/D-2f froze — defined ONCE in the targeting module, consumed everywhere (FR38, AD-14).** A monster is a valid target wherever **either** of its cells qualifies: **melee** reaches it at its **front-most** occupied row (and it **blocks both rows of its column** for the FR8 nearest-row blockade — a melee unit can never strike past it); **ranged** reaches it at its **rear-most** occupied row (rearmost eligibility); the **blast** counts it in **each** row it occupies when choosing the "row with most living enemies," and a blast that strikes a row the monster occupies **hits it once** (row-scoped effects hit a monster once — PRD assumption CONFIRMED). Smalls are unchanged (front row === rear row). No shell code re-derives any of this (AD-2).

5. **Two-cell ACTING semantics read the ANCHOR (FR38, D-2f, FR13).** A monster always acts **from its anchor row** (the front-most cell): its **action count** (`actions[anchorRow]`), its **move-kind**, its **reach** (from the anchor column), and **FR13's front-before-back tie-break** all read the anchor — no special cases. (`UnitState.rowIndex`/`colIndex` = the anchor already, so acting is largely free; the work is AC4's targeting.)

6. **AI, harness, and determinism cover monster armies (FR24, FR25, NFR4, FR20, AD-8).** `STRATEGY_POOL` gains **1-monster and 2-monster** archetypes (slot-legal: e.g. Golem + 3 smalls = 2+1+1+1; two Golems + 1 small = 2+2+1), the archetype `classes`/`placement` shapes become **variable-length** (a Golem army is 4 or 3 units, not 5), and `chooseSetup`'s leader draw + mirror flip stay correct for monster armies (the mirror must not break the never-share-a-column rule). The `matchSetupArb` property arbitrary generates **slot-legal monster armies with valid anchor placements**; the both-mode sim sweep runs monster comps and stays in the **≤65% band** (Golem HP 300 is the first tuning target — re-tune data only if a comp breaks out; record converged numbers, keep the wardens melee-viability floor honest). Exhaustive unit tests cover each AC-4/AC-5 semantic and each AC-3 validation error; property tests hold over monster armies; **goldens include a monster battle**; `balanceVersion` bumps. `docs/rules.md` gains a "Monsters" note (the story-2.4 drift guard stays green).

### THE TWO-CELL SEMANTICS (dossier §2 / D-2f — the epic's core engine task, DECIDED)
Two rules, one direction each (AD-14: implemented ONCE in the targeting module, consumed everywhere):
- **TARGETED:** the Golem is a valid target wherever **either** cell qualifies — melee at its **front** cell, ranged at its **rear** cell, blast in **each** occupied row. It **blocks both rows of its column** for the melee FR8 blockade. **Row-scoped effects hit it once** (PRD assumption CONFIRMED). For the blast's "row with most living enemies" count, it **counts in each row it occupies**.
- **ACTS:** always **from its anchor** (the front-most cell) — action count, move-kind, and FR13's front-before-back tie-break all read the anchor. No special cases.

The clean engine model (recommended): `TargetCandidate`/`UnitState` gain a `rearRowIndex` (front = `rowIndex`, rear = `rowIndex + 1` for a monster, else equal). Melee reads front (`rowIndex`), ranged reads rear (`rearRowIndex`), blast counts both. A monster is still ONE candidate → hit once by identity. Acting reads `rowIndex` (= anchor) unchanged. [Source: DOSSIER.md#§2, D-2f; ARCHITECTURE-SPINE.md#AD-14]

### AD-14 — one unit, one id, one anchor, a DERIVED footprint
A monster is one `side:index` id; its placement stores ONE **anchor** = the front-most cell; the second cell is derived via `footprintCells(class, anchor)` and **never stored**. Anchor `front` → {front, mid}; anchor `mid` → {mid, back}; anchor **`back` is illegal** (body leaves the grid). Every consumer — validation, targeting, and (story 4.9) the renderer/History — calls that ONE helper; nothing re-derives the footprint. Every `BattleLog` event references the unit id, never a cell (already true — a monster changes no event shape). [Source: ARCHITECTURE-SPINE.md#AD-14; epics.md#AD-14]

### Placement legality has ONE implementation (AD-14) — do NOT fork it
`legalAnchors`/`canPlace` (built here for story 4.9's live drag feedback) MUST share their core predicate with `validateMatchSetup` — factor the per-unit + cross-unit legality into a shared internal function, expose the incremental helpers over it. The strongest test of the "one implementation" contract is a property: for any army + anchor, `canPlace(...)` is true **iff** the resulting full `MatchSetup` passes `validateMatchSetup`. FR4's monster rules (F+M or M+B, never horizontal, ≤2 monsters, no shared column, no overlap) live in exactly one place. [Source: ARCHITECTURE-SPINE.md#AD-14; epics.md Story 4.8 AC1; validate.ts:127-150]

### The Golem move-kind — a small dev default (non-blocking)

The Golem is "everyone else" → a uniform melee kind across all three rows. `MoveKind` = `slash | arrow | blast | staff | bash`. ### Device-reported follow-up (2026-07-19) — TWO new engine rules + a minimal shell patch
Danilo's own testing surfaced a real design gap alongside the shell bug above: **two monsters standing in neighboring (not just the same) columns should be illegal** — their two-cell bodies would visibly touch — and **a monster must never be crownable as the leader** (the leader-fall sober package and Attack-Leader both treat the leader as a single vulnerable unit; a 2-cell wall surviving as its own army's leader defeats the mechanic). Both are NEW `validateMatchSetup` rules, not bugs in the original AC3/AC-14 footprint work:
- `'monsters-share-column'` was RENAMED to **`'monsters-too-close'`** and its check widened from column-EQUALITY to column-ADJACENCY (`|colIndexA − colIndexB| ≤ 1`) — on the 3-column grid this means 2 monsters are legal ONLY at left+right (center is adjacent to both). `MAX_MONSTERS_PER_ARMY = 2` is now an exported `balance.ts` constant (was an inline literal in two places) so the engine's cap and the Draft shell's slot-gating can never drift apart.
- A new **`'monster-cannot-lead'`** violation: the existing leader-bounds-check loop in `validateMatchSetup` now also rejects a leader index whose class is `sizeClass: 'monster'`.
- `chooseSetup`'s leader draw (`ai.ts`) now draws ONLY from eligible (non-monster) indices — still exactly ONE stream draw per call either way (mapped through a filtered index list, never a redraw-on-reject), so a monster-FREE archetype's draw is bit-identical to before this fix; only a monster-containing archetype's leader distribution actually changes.
- `matchSetupArb` (`test/arbitraries.ts`) needed BOTH fixes: monster columns are now drawn from a dedicated `monsterColsArb` (0/1 monsters: any column; 2 monsters: always the {left,right} pair, order randomized) instead of an unconstrained `shuffledSubarray`, and the leader index is now drawn only from small-class indices (`smallCount` is always ≥1, so this never draws from an empty set).
- Every hand-built engine fixture that happened to default its leader to army index 0 while that index was a Golem (`validate.test.ts`'s `monsterSetup()`, several `monster.test.ts` battles, golden #11) needed an explicit non-monster `leaders` override — a mechanical but wide-reaching ripple, since the bug was "index 0 is the crown default" colliding with "index 0 is also where every monster.test.ts fixture happens to put its Golem."

The shell patch (kept intentionally minimal — full two-cell rendering stays 4.9's scope):
- `packages/engine/src/validate.ts`/`index.ts`: exported `MAX_MONSTERS_PER_ARMY`; `canPlace`/`legalAnchors` automatically inherit both new rules since they share `footprintViolation` with `validateMatchSetup` (AD-14 — no second implementation needed).
- `draftModel.ts`'s `canAddUnit(army, cls)` gained the candidate class as a REQUIRED second parameter (was army-only, so it could only ever check the running total, never what THIS unit would cost or whether a monster cap was already hit) — checks both the candidate's own slot cost against the remaining budget AND the monster count against `MAX_MONSTERS_PER_ARMY`. `DraftScene.ts`'s two call sites updated.
- `placement.ts`'s pure model gained a REQUIRED `classes` parameter throughout and now calls the engine's own `canPlace` to decide every drop's legality (AD-14 — no second implementation in the shell either) — an illegal drop is REJECTED (same board reference returned, so the scene can detect a no-op) rather than silently accepted and left to fail much later at `commit()`. The one exception, preserved for UX: a clean SMALL-for-SMALL swap at the exact anchor cell (both are single-cell footprints, so it can never produce an illegal board). New `occupiedCells(board, classes)` export gives the scene every reserved cell (anchor + derived) for rendering.
- `MatchFlow.placeUnit` now threads `playerArmy`'s classes through to the model; `MatchFlow.setLeader` throws if the target index's class is a monster (mirrors `validateMatchSetup`'s new rule at the point of the gesture, not just at commit).
- `PlacementScene.ts`: the drop handler and the double-tap-to-place path (`firstFreeCell`, REWRITTEN to call `legalAnchors` directly instead of hand-rolling an anchor-only "taken cells" set — the exact same class of bug as the drop rejection) now surface a transient on-screen toast on rejection instead of failing silently; the crown-tap handler catches `setLeader`'s throw and toasts instead of propagating; the commit button wraps `flow.commit()` in try/catch and toasts the error instead of leaving a console-only failure with a stuck Ready button. ### Device-reported follow-up, ROUND 2 (2026-07-19, same day) — the adjacency ban is broader than round 1 shipped
Danilo confirmed the exact rule against the SOURCE GAME itself (a screenshot of the actual mechanic firing, treated as the highest-authority evidence per his standing OB64-fidelity instruction — a prose "guide" pasted earlier turned out to be WRONG on this point): **"You cannot position other characters next to large characters."** Round 1 (above) only banned a SECOND MONSTER from standing in the same-or-adjacent column. The confirmed rule is broader: **NO unit — human or monster — may occupy a cell horizontally adjacent, in the same row, to any monster's footprint cell.** This fully subsumes round 1's monster-vs-monster check (two monsters in adjacent columns always share at least one row, since each occupies 2 of the 3 rows) and ALSO bans a small from standing beside a monster — which round 1 explicitly allowed and Danilo's own Placement screenshot showed happening (a Knight crowned leader, standing at front/center, directly between two Golems at front/left and front/right).
- `footprintViolation` (`validate.ts`) rewritten around a general two-way cell-adjacency check instead of a monster-column-index comparison: a running `monsterBannedCells` set (seeded by each processed monster's footprint, expanded to its horizontally-adjacent same-row cells) rejects ANY later unit — small or monster — landing on a banned cell; a symmetric check (when the CURRENT unit is itself a monster) rejects it if its own ban zone would hit a cell an EARLIER unit already occupies. This replaces the simpler column-index-diff check entirely — `monsterColIndices` is gone. The violation code is renamed again, `'monsters-too-close'` → **`'adjacent-to-monster'`**, since it's no longer monster-specific.
- Consequence for legal 2-monster armies: the center column is now banned in EVERY row either monster's body reaches — if both Golems share the same anchor row (both front-anchored, or both mid-anchored), only ONE row of center stays open; if their anchors differ, all three rows of center end up banned. Neither case makes a "2 monsters + 1 small" army uncompletable on this 3×3 grid, though — left/right columns each still have one cell the OWNING monster doesn't occupy (its "third" row), so the mandatory 3rd unit (5-slot budget − 2 golems × 2 slots = exactly 1 small) always has at least one legal cell even in the worst case. (An earlier framing of this consequence during scoping overstated it as potentially making a squad impossible — corrected here; the strict rule never blocks a legal army from being completable, it only removes center as an option.)
- `ai.ts`'s `golem-wall` archetype needed a second fix: its witch (previously at mid/left, beside the front-center-anchored Golem) is now at back/center — the Golem's own front+mid footprint bans mid/left and mid/right for anyone, human included, so every non-Golem slot in this archetype now sits in the back row. `twin-golems` (both Golems front-anchored at left/right, cleric at back/center) needed no change — matching anchor rows was already its shape, and back/center was never in either Golem's ban zone.
- `test/arbitraries.ts`'s generator was simplified to lean on the engine instead of re-deriving the rule a second time: `armyAndPlacementsArb` now calls the engine's own `legalAnchors(smallClass, monsterUnitsSoFar)` to compute the free-cell pool for smalls, rather than hand-filtering `ALL_CELLS` by monster footprint alone (which would now under-reject and generate invalid boards). This is a smaller, more AD-14-faithful generator than round 1's version.
- Every hand-built fixture that placed ANY unit (not just a second monster) in a cell adjacent to a Golem needed rework — this was a much wider ripple than round 1's leader-index fixes, since round 1 explicitly allowed small-beside-monster placements as filler. Every `monster.test.ts` battle, golden #11, and one `match-flow.test.ts`/`placement.test.ts` fixture moved its "filler" small(s) out of the newly-banned cells (typically into the monster's own back row, or a genuinely non-adjacent column). Golden #11 specifically could no longer keep its "A's back row stays empty so the Golem's rear cell is the deepest occupied cell" premise — with a Golem-plus-3-smalls army, at least one small is now FORCED into the back row regardless of the Golem's column (no non-back-row cells are left over once one edge column and the always-banned center are accounted for) — so the golden's scope narrowed to blockade + acts-from-anchor only, with the "ranged reaches the monster's rear cell" and "blast double-count decides a tie" proofs now living purely in `monster.test.ts`'s dedicated (already-passing) fixtures, which don't carry the same full-5-slot-army constraint.
- New tests added for the exact confirmed scenario: `validate.test.ts` gained "rejects a HUMAN standing directly beside a monster"; `placement.test.ts` gained the mirror at the shell-model layer. Both directly replay the Jo-Ann-beside-the-Hellhound screenshot's shape.
- `docs/rules.md`'s Monsters section restates the rule in the broader "no unit — human or monster" form; `rules-doc.test.ts`'s pin updated to match (the round-1 pinned phrase no longer appears verbatim in the doc).

### Device-reported follow-up, ROUND 3 (2026-07-19, same day) — dropping a monster on the BACK row was wrongly rejected outright

- Two existing tests (`placement.test.ts`, `match-flow.test.ts`) that had asserted the OLD "reject a back-row drop" behavior were rewritten to assert the new re-anchor-to-mid behavior instead — this was a genuine behavior change, not a new gap being closed, so those specific assertions were wrong, not just incomplete.
