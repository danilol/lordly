## What went well

- **The choke-point bet paid off** — 3.1's `recordResult()` seam absorbed 3.2's entire replay feature with zero ResultScene changes (byte-identical-storage proven); AD-2 log-player purity gave replay speed/skip for free.
- **Determinism compounds** — 3.2's Replay unexpectedly became 3.4's reproducible worst-case benchmark instrument. Nobody planned that seam; FR20 keeps paying unplanned dividends.
- **The sweep beat reasoning twice** (3.0) — v1's wipeout three-mages dominance (74.6%) AND the approved fix's single-mode failure (archer walls ~75%) were both caught empirically; systematic attribution led to a PO-approved mode-scoped attenuation, mid-story, without derailing.
- **Danilo's verdict on the epic's centerpiece:** "The replay and the history are great features that will enable players improve their games, and learn with mistakes. I loved them."
- **PO amendments landed mid-flight without friction** — the history mode-tag ask shipped same-day during 3.1's review; the spec-is-binding rule held.

## Challenges / lessons

- **The 3.4 instrument fiasco (the epic's big lesson):** the fps sampler shipped with three defects — it recorded a once-per-second EMA while claiming per-frame precision, leaked a duplicate listener per scene re-entry, and blessed a "raw trace" of 5,746 samples in a 3,600-cap buffer. The story record said "AC1 CLOSED" on top of it. The adversarial review caught all three pre-sign-off; sampler rebuilt (per-frame `1000/rawDelta`, shutdown detach, cap 36k), Danilo re-captured same day (9,380 samples, zero floor breaches), AC1 closed on sound evidence. **We pointed empiricism at the game and never at the meter.** Danilo's take: "we're doing pretty good. Not a major problem. We learned with the mistake!" — correct: nothing false reached prod; the system worked.
- **The singleton-scene trap keeps changing costumes** — memorized in 2.2 as "reset transient fields", it returned as a double-tap transition (3.2 replay button) and an accumulating event listener (3.4 sampler). The checklist entry was too narrow, not wrong.
- **Config lies until you read its artifact** — 3.3's `autoUpdate` was configured and completely inert (`injectRegister: 'inline'` silently suppressed skipWaiting); icons weren't git-tracked. Both caught only by reviewers reading `dist/sw.js` and doing clean rebuilds.
- **Balance watch item for Epic 4:** melee-witch comps are the new floor (wardens 33% single-mode) — Danilo's "melee too weak" instinct, empirically located; tactics (attack-weakest) is the fix, and it's Epic 4 territory.

## Team agreements (new)

- **Verify the meter, not just the game** — any measurement instrument or build-config claim is checked against the artifact it produces (read the generated output, adversarially test the sampler) before its numbers enter a permanent record.
- **Singleton-scene rule generalized** — audit everything `create()` registers: fields, listeners, AND transitions, with cleanup on shutdown. The costume changes; the bug doesn't.
- **Evidence provenance** — a number in a permanent record must be reproducible by committed tooling.
